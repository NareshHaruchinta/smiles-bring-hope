Our Journey Support Our Mission

Founded 2009

A Vision Born From Compassion

Smiles Bring Hope Foundation was founded on a simple but powerful belief: every child deserves access to quality dental care, regardless of their family's financial circumstances.

Dr. Edith Smail and Dr. Douglas Smail saw firsthand how untreated dental disease affected the confidence, academic performance, and overall wellbeing of children in underserved communities. They witnessed the transformative power of a single act of kindness — a professional cleaning, a filling, a reason to smile without shame.

Together with a growing network of volunteer dentists, dental hygienists, and compassionate community members, they established a nonprofit dedicated to closing the dental care gap. What started as a small initiative has evolved into a thriving organization serving hundreds of children annually.

Today, Smiles Bring Hope remains rooted in that original vision: compassionate care, community partnership, and the belief that every smile matters.

Our Journey

Milestones of Impact

From humble beginnings to meaningful scale — our growth reflects the dedication of our community.

2009: Foundation Founded

Dr. Edith Smail and Dr. Douglas Smail establish Smiles Bring Hope Foundation with a vision to provide free dental care to underserved children.

2012: First Community Center Partnership

We expand our reach beyond direct care to include dental health education and community outreach programs.

2015: Volunteer Program Launch

Recognizing the power of community, we formally establish our volunteer program, welcoming dental professionals and students.

2018: Faith-Based Partnerships

We deepen our impact through strategic partnerships with faith communities, reaching families that traditional health systems overlook.

2021: 500+ Children Served

Our cumulative impact reaches a major milestone — we've restored 500+ smiles and counting.

2024–Present: Expanding Nationally

We're scaling our model to additional communities, bringing our mission of compassionate dental care to more children nationwide.

Why It Matters

A Smile Is More Than a Smile

When a child lives with untreated dental disease, they often live with pain. But the damage goes deeper. They miss school days. They withdraw from class participation. They avoid smiling, laughing, speaking up. Bit by bit, their confidence erodes at exactly the age when it's being formed.

Our philosophy is simple: When we restore a smile, we restore possibility. We give a child permission to shine. We send a message that says, "You matter. Your health matters. Your future matters."

That single shift — from shame to confidence — ripples outward. It shows up in raised hands during class. In friendships formed. In dreams pursued without hesitation. In a child who knows, at a fundamental level, that they are worthy of care.

That's why we do this work. One smile, multiplied across hundreds of children and families, is the revolution we're building.
